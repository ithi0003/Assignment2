Australian Bushfires

A compact look at satellite-detected fire activity across Australia. The map shows where hotspots occur and how intense they are; the time series shows how hotspot counts evolve by day and night. Data source: NASA FIRMS (Kaggle), Aug - Sep 2019 sample used for this dashboard.

Hotspot Map

Each circle marks a satellite hotspot detection plotted at its latitude/longitude. Colour encodes Fire Radiative Power (FRP) and size reflects the sensor scan value. The base map provides geographic context (ocean, country outline, state borders and graticules).

  • What you see: Clusters of hotspots along northern and eastern regions during the sample period.
  • Read the colours: Darker hues indicate higher FRP (hotter, more energetic fires).
  • Hover anywhere: Tooltips reveal exact date, FRP, brightness and scan size for that detection.

Tip: Use this view to spot regional concentrations and unusually intense hotspots.

Hotspot Counts Over Time (Day vs Night)

Daily hotspot counts are stacked by the Day/Night flag to show both the total volume and each period's contribution. Use the dropdown to focus on Day only or Night only. A dashed rule marks the peak total day.

  • Interaction: Dropdown filter (All / D / N) and tooltips on hover.
  • Annotation: The dashed vertical line identifies the highest daily total; label shows the count.
  • Key takeaway: Daytime detections dominate overall volume, while short Night spikes still occur near peak activity windows.

Insight: Peaks align with dry, windy periods; counts recede after cooler or wetter intervals.

Source: NASA FIRMS (via Kaggle). Visualisations built with Vega-Lite.
