Australian Bushfires

A data-driven exploration of Australia's bushfire activity using NASA FIRMS satellite hotspot data (Aug-Sep 2019). The first visualisation maps hotspots by location and intensity; the second tracks daily hotspot counts, comparing day and night detections to reveal temporal patterns in fire activity.

Hotspot Map

Each circle represents a satellite-detected fire hotspot. Colour encodes Fire Radiative Power (FRP) (energy released), while circle size reflects the sensor scan value. The basemap provides geographic context with ocean, country outline, state borders and graticules.

  • Clustering: Dense hotspots along northern and eastern Australia during the sample period.
  • Colour scale: Soft yellow -> deep orange indicates higher FRP (more energetic fires).
  • Hover: Tooltips show date, brightness, FRP and scan size for each detection.

Use this view to identify regions with concentrated and intense fire activity.

Hotspot Counts Over Time (Day vs Night)

Daily hotspot counts are stacked by the Day/Night flag to show total volume and each period's contribution. A dropdown filter lets you focus on Day or Night only. A dashed rule marks the peak day of activity.

  • Interaction: Dropdown filter (All / Day (D) / Night (N)) and tooltips on hover.
  • Annotation: Dashed vertical rule + label highlights the highest daily total.
  • Takeaway: Daytime detections dominate overall volume; short Night spikes occur around peak windows.

Patterns line up with hot, dry, windy intervals; counts recede after cooler or wetter periods.
