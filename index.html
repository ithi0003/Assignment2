Hotspot Intensity and Distribution

About the Map

This interactive map visualises satellite-detected fire hotspots across Australia. Each circle marks a detection, with colour intensity representing Fire Radiative Power (FRP) - a measure of fire strength - and size corresponding to scan width.

  • Fire clusters appear along northern and eastern regions.
  • Higher FRP values occur where drought is prolonged.
  • Hover to reveal fire details such as brightness and energy.

Fire Risk by Month and State

About the Heatmap

This heatmap visualises fire risk intensity across Australian states throughout the year. Each cell's colour shows relative fire danger from 0 (low) to 1 (high).

  • Fire risk peaks in summer months (Dec-Feb).
  • Southern states stay cooler with lower risk.
  • NT & QLD show long high-risk dry seasons.

Forest Area vs Burnt Area

About the Chart

This chart compares each state's total forest area with the burnt portion during the 2019-2020 bushfire season.

  • Each bar = total native forest area (in thousand hectares).
  • Red shows burnt portion; teal shows remaining unburnt area.
  • NSW and Victoria experienced the largest losses - over 6 million ha combined burnt.
  • WA and QLD had extensive forests but relatively low burnt percentages.
  • ACT had the highest proportion burnt relative to size (around 64%).
  • Nationally, about 6% of all native forests were affected.
  • Hover to see exact values per state.
